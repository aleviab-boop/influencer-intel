# Competitive Teardown Scorecard

**Goal.** Sign up for trials of the four key competitors, run a real D2C campaign brief through each, document where each fails. Output: a sales-deck-grade comparison + clearer view of where to play.

**Tools to test:**

1. Qoruz (qoruz.com)
2. Plixxo (plixxo.com)
3. Winkl (winkl.co)
4. Modash (modash.io)

Optional benchmarking:

- CreatorIQ (enterprise — may not give trial)
- HypeAuditor (audience-only tool, useful as a credibility benchmark)

---

## The test brief

Use a real brief from a design-partner conversation. If none yet, use this:

> Festive (Diwali) campaign for a mid-priced ghee-based skincare brand. Budget ₹8L total. Target: women 25–34, Mumbai + Bangalore + Delhi. Want creators with past festive content and ≥85% follower credibility. Need 20–30 shortlisted, ranked. Bonus if regional/vernacular creators are in the mix.

Run this exact brief on every tool. Time the workflow.

---

## Scoring dimensions (1–5 scale, 5 = best)

### Data quality (40%)

| # | Dimension |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | Total India creator coverage (estimated) |
| 2 | Tier-2/3 creator depth |
| 3 | Vernacular coverage (Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Marathi, Kannada) |
| 4 | Audience demographic completeness (gender split, top cities, age bands) |
| 5 | Demographic data freshness (when last refreshed) |
| 6 | YouTube creator coverage |

### Credibility & trust (20%)

| # | Dimension |
|---|-----------|
| 7 | Native credibility / fake-follower score (proprietary or resold?) |
| 8 | Audience overlap detection |
| 9 | Brand-safety screening |
| 10 | Transparent reasoning per creator |

### Workflow & UX (20%)

| # | Dimension |
|---|-----------|
| 11 | Time from login to first shortlist (seconds) |
| 12 | Brief input — chat / form / filters? |
| 13 | Number of fields/clicks to filter |
| 14 | Shortlist comparison view |
| 15 | Excel export quality |

### Agentic features (10%)

| # | Dimension |
|---|-----------|
| 16 | Outreach drafting |
| 17 | Reply tracking / inbox |
| 18 | Performance loop (post-campaign tracking) |

### Pricing & access (10%)

| # | Dimension |
|---|-----------|
| 19 | Pricing transparency (public or quote-only) |
| 20 | Lowest tier price (₹/mo) |
| 21 | Free trial or freemium tier |
| 22 | Onboarding friction (sales call required?) |

---

## For each tool, capture

1. **Screenshots** — hero, brief input, shortlist view, creator detail, export
2. **Workflow video** — 3-min Loom walking through brief→shortlist
3. **One-line strengths** (3 max)
4. **One-line weaknesses** (3 max)
5. **Killer-quote moment** — the worst UX failure or data gap (used in sales deck)
6. **Estimated coverage gap vs target** (50K+ with vernacular ≥40%)

---

## Output deliverable

A 6-page deck:

- **p1** — The brief used
- **p2–5** — Per-tool side-by-side score + screenshots + the killer quote
- **p6** — Where Influencer Intel plays — white-space chart with us in the open seam (India-deep data + AI-native + mid-market priced)

This deck = sales document for design-partner asks AND framing artefact for the founding team.

---

## Watch-outs

- Don't game the brief to your strengths. Use a real brand brief. If our tool doesn't beat the incumbent on a real brief, the wedge isn't real.
- Note where competitors are *better* — those are real risks, not just gaps.
- Don't rely on G2 reviews or marketing pages. Sign up, run the brief, capture screenshots.
