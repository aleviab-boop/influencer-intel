# Customer Discovery Script

**Goal.** 15 calls × 30 min with marketing leads at D2C brands (₹5–500 Cr revenue, runs ≥2 influencer campaigns/quarter). Validate the wedge (India-deep data + credibility + brief-to-shortlist). Capture pricing willingness. Ask for design-partner intros.

**Anti-goal.** Pitching the product. Mom Test rules apply — talk about their life, ask about past specifics, listen 70% of the time.

---

## Booking the call

Cold email / LinkedIn (founder → brand marketing lead):

> **Subject:** 25-min chat — how Indian D2C brands run influencer campaigns
>
> Hi [Name],
>
> I'm researching how in-house brand teams in India run influencer campaigns end-to-end — discovery, vetting, outreach, post-campaign learning. Not selling anything. I'm building something in the space and want to understand the pain before designing the solution.
>
> Would you have 25 minutes this/next week to walk me through how your last campaign actually ran? Happy to share what I've heard from other brands at the end.
>
> [Calendar link]
>
> [Founder]

---

## On the call

### Opening (2 min)

> Thanks for the time. Quick context — I'm researching the influencer marketing workflow at D2C brands in India. I want to understand how it actually works, not pitch you anything. I'll mostly be listening. Cool to record this for my notes?

### Section 1 — Their world (5 min)

- How many campaigns did you run last quarter?
- What format mix (sponsored posts, reels, longer collabs)?
- Who's involved — internal team, agency, freelancer?
- What's the typical budget range per campaign?

### Section 2 — Last campaign walkthrough (8 min)

> Walk me through your last campaign, start to finish. Don't summarise — tell me what actually happened day by day.

Probe:
- How did you decide which influencers to consider?
- Where did the names come from — agency, your own list, search?
- How did you decide which ones were credible?
- How did outreach work — who messaged, on what channel?
- How did you negotiate rates?
- After the campaign, how did you measure if it worked?

### Section 3 — Pain points (5 min)

- What was the worst part of that campaign?
- What surprised you afterwards (good or bad)?
- Any time in the last year you got burned — fake followers, ghost engagement, paid post that flopped?
- If you could have a magic wand for one part of this workflow, where would it go?

### Section 4 — Tools they tried (3 min)

- Have you used Qoruz / Plixxo / Winkl / Modash / CreatorIQ?
- What worked, what didn't?
- What did you stop using and why?
- Anything you currently pay for? How much per month?

### Section 5 — What "good" looks like (3 min)

- Imagine the ideal version of this — what would it do for you?
- What would have to be true for you to try a new tool?

### Section 6 — Pricing signal (Van Westendorp, 2 min)

> Hypothetical tool: takes a brief, returns 30 ranked creators with credibility scores, drafts personalised outreach. Indian-creator depth with vernacular coverage. Used by your team end-to-end.
>
> 1. At what monthly price would this be **so cheap you'd doubt the quality**?
> 2. At what price would this be a **bargain — clearly worth it**?
> 3. At what price would this start to feel **expensive but you'd still consider**?
> 4. At what price would this be **so expensive you wouldn't buy**?

Capture all four numbers per respondent.

### Section 7 — Close (2 min)

- Would you be open to being a design partner — free 6 months, weekly 30-min feedback, case study rights?
- Anyone else in your network running into the same problems I should talk to?
- Can I update you when there's something to show?

---

## After every call (5 min)

Capture in `discovery-notes.md`:

1. **Quote of the call** — their exact words on the worst pain point
2. **Top 3 features they'd pay for tomorrow** (in their words, not yours)
3. **Tools they're actively paying for, and price**
4. **Van Westendorp scores** (4 numbers)
5. **Did they ask "where can I sign up?"** — yes/no
6. **Intro asks** — names + email/LinkedIn

---

## After 15 calls — pattern doc

Synthesise:

- The three pains that came up >10 times
- The features named >5 times as "would pay for"
- Tools currently paid for (incumbent landscape)
- Pricing band (Van Westendorp optimal price point — see `pricing-test-framework.md`)
- Design-partner LOI count (target: 5)

If <5 LOIs after 15 calls, the wedge isn't sharp enough. Re-think positioning before continuing.
